Nest

Black-capped Chickadees are cavity nesters. They will either dig out their own nest in a tree or use one that is already there. The females tend to pick the spot and the males will help excavate. This creates a protected place for them to raise their young. After the hole is created (averages at about 21cm deep) the female will make the inside round and layer it with leaves, feathers, fur, etc.

Clutch

Black-capped Chickadees will have a clutch that ranges from 1 - 13 eggs. The eggs will incubate for 12 - 13 days. During the incubation period, the male chickadee will gather food and feed the female.

Hatching

After the eggs hatch, the male will feed the family. Then, as the baby birds begin to grow bigger, the female will help the male feed the babies. The baby chickadees will remain in the nest for 12 - 16 days.

Leaving the Nest

Once the chickadees are old enough, they will leave the nest. They will remain with their parents for about 2 - 3 weeks. After that, they go off by themselves and start their own families. They usually only have one clutch because most live for 1.5 years.
